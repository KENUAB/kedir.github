School Of Addis abeba

General Information

Ababa, the capital city of Ethiopia, hosts a variety of schools ranging from primary to secondary and higher education institutions. These schools include public, private, and international options, catering to the diverse needs of its population.

    Types of Schools

  • Public Schools: Funded and run by the government, these schools follow the national curriculum set by the Ministry of Education.
    • Private Schools: These are independently run and may offer different curricula, including national and international programs.
  • International Schools: Cater to expatriates and locals seeking an international curriculum, such as the International Baccalaureate (IB), Cambridge International Examinations (CIE), or American curricula.

      4. Admissions

      1. Schools: Typically based on catchment areas. Admission may require proof of residence and completion of the previous grade level.
      2. Private and International Schools: Often have more detailed admission processes including entrance exams, interviews, and submission of previous academic records. It is advisable to check each school's specific requirements and deadlines.

        Curriculum

      1. National Curriculum: Implemented by public schools and many private schools, covering subjects like Amharic, English, mathematics, science, and social studies.
      2. Curricula: Offered by international schools, including IB, CIE, and American curricula. These programs are recognized globally and often emphasize critical thinking, creativity, and global awareness.
